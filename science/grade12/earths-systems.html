The $\text{Four}$ $\text{Spheres}$ $\text{Defined}$ 📏

 
   

1. $\text{Geosphere}$ ($\text{Geo} = \text{Earth}$)

   

The $\text{solid}$ $\text{Earth}$: $\text{core}$, $\text{mantle}$, and $\text{crust}$. Includes $\text{rocks}$, $\text{minerals}$, and $\text{landforms}$.

   

$\text{Key}$ $\text{Process}$: $\text{Plate}$ $\text{Tectonics}$, $\text{Volcanism}$.

 
 
   

2. $\text{Hydrosphere}$ ($\text{Hydro} = \text{Water}$)

   

All the $\text{water}$ on $\text{Earth}$: $\text{oceans}$, $\text{lakes}$, $\text{rivers}$, $\text{glaciers}$, and $\text{groundwater}$.

   

$\text{Key}$ $\text{Process}$: $\text{Water}$ $\text{Cycle}$, $\text{Ocean}$ $\text{Currents}$.

 
 
   

3. $\text{Atmosphere}$ ($\text{Atmo} = \text{Vapour}$)

   

The $\text{layer}$ of $\text{gases}$ surrounding the $\text{planet}$. Includes $\text{weather}$ and $\text{climate}$.

   

$\text{Key}$ $\text{Process}$: $\text{Convection}$, $\text{Greenhouse}$ $\text{Effect}$.

 
 
   

4. $\text{Biosphere}$ ($\text{Bio} = \text{Life}$)

   

All $\text{living}$ $\text{things}$ and the $\text{regions}$ of $\text{Earth}$ where $\text{life}$ $\text{exists}$.

   

$\text{Key}$ $\text{Process}$: $\text{Photosynthesis}$, $\text{Respiration}$.

 

Interactions: $\text{Biogeochemical}$ $\text{Cycles}$ 🔄

  The spheres are connected through major global cycles that move essential elements through $\text{abiotic}$ (non-living) and $\text{biotic}$ (living) parts of the system.

 
   

$\text{Carbon}$ $\text{Cycle}$

   

The movement of $\text{carbon}$ between the $\text{atmosphere}$ ($\text{CO}_2$), $\text{hydrosphere}$ (dissolved $\text{CO}_2$), $\text{biosphere}$ (organic $\text{matter}$), and $\text{geosphere}$ ($\text{fossil}$ $\text{fuels}$ and $\text{rocks}$).

   

$$\text{Example}: \text{Volcanoes}$ $\text{release}$ $\text{CO}_2$ ($\text{Geo} \to \text{Atmo}$).

 
 
   

$\text{Water}$ $\text{Cycle}$

   

The continuous movement of $\text{water}$ on, above, and below the $\text{surface}$ of the $\text{Earth}$.

   

$$\text{Example}: \text{Evaporation}$ $\text{from}$ $\text{oceans}$ ($\text{Hydro} \to \text{Atmo}$).
