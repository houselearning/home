Ecology: $\text{Levels}$ $\text{of}$ $\text{Organization}$ 🔬

  **$\text{Ecology}$** is the study of the relationships between $\text{living}$ $\text{organisms}$ and their $\text{physical}$ $\text{environment}$.

 
   

$\text{Population}$

   

A $\text{group}$ of $\text{individuals}$ of the $\text{same}$ $\text{species}$ in the $\text{same}$ $\text{area}$.

 
 
   

$\text{Community}$

   

All the $\text{different}$ $\text{populations}$ of $\text{organisms}$ living in an $\text{area}$. ($\text{Biotic}$ only).

 
 
   

$\text{Ecosystem}$

   

The $\text{community}$ ($\text{biotic}$) $\text{interacting}$ with the $\text{abiotic}$ $\text{environment}$ ($\text{water}$, $\text{soil}$).

 
 
   

$\text{Biosphere}$

   

The $\text{sum}$ of all $\text{living}$ $\text{matter}$ on $\text{Earth}$ where $\text{life}$ $\text{exists}$.

Energy $\text{Flow}$: $\text{Trophic}$ $\text{Levels}$ ⬆️

  $\text{Energy}$ $\text{flows}$ through an $\text{ecosystem}$ starting with $\text{solar}$ $\text{energy}$, and is categorized into $\text{Trophic}$ $\text{Levels}$.

 
   

1. $\text{Producers}$ ($\text{Autotrophs}$)

   

Organisms that $\text{make}$ their $\text{own}$ $\text{food}$ ($\text{e.g.,}$ $\text{plants}$).

 
 
   

2. $\text{Primary}$ $\text{Consumers}$ ($\text{Herbivores}$)

   

Eat $\text{producers}$ ($\text{e.g.,}$ $\text{deer}$).

 
 
   

3. $\text{Secondary}$ $\text{Consumers}$ ($\text{Carnivores}/\text{Omnivores}$)

   

Eat $\text{primary}$ $\text{consumers}$ ($\text{e.g.,}$ $\text{fox}$).

 
 
   

$\text{The}$ $\text{10}\%$ $\text{Rule}$

   

Only about **$\mathbf{10}\%$ $\text{of}$ $\text{the}$ $\text{energy}$** from one $\text{trophic}$ $\text{level}$ is $\text{transferred}$ to the $\text{next}$; the rest is $\text{lost}$ as $\text{heat}$.

 

Biomes: $\text{Climate}$ $\text{and}$ $\text{Life}$ 🌍

  A **$\text{Biome}$** is a large $\text{regional}$ $\text{ecosystem}$ characterized by $\text{distinct}$ $\text{climate}$ $\text{and}$ $\text{plant}$ $\text{life}$. $\text{Climate}$ is the $\text{primary}$ $\text{factor}$ determining which $\text{biome}$ develops.

 
   

1. $\text{Temperature}$

   

The $\text{average}$ $\text{yearly}$ $\text{temperature}$ is critical. $\text{Determines}$ $\text{growing}$ $\text{season}$ $\text{length}$ and $\text{rates}$ of $\text{decay}$.

 
 
   

2. $\text{Precipitation}$

   

The $\text{total}$ $\text{yearly}$ $\text{rainfall}/\text{snowfall}$. $\text{Limits}$ the $\text{biomass}$ $\text{that}$ $\text{can}$ $\text{grow}$.

 
 
   

$\text{Examples}$ $\text{of}$ $\text{Biomes}$

   

$\text{Tundra}$ ($\text{low}$ $T$, $\text{low}$ $\text{P}$), $\text{Tropical}$ $\text{Rain}$ $\text{Forest}$ ($\text{high}$ $T$, $\text{high}$ $\text{P}$), $\text{Desert}$ ($\text{variable}$ $T$, $\text{very}$ $\text{low}$ $\text{P}$).
